Respect People, not Beliefs

All people were created equal, not so with beliefs.

Posted by Bing L. Cherry on February 27, 2021

First, to make things clear, I am not claiming that people should be disrespected or mistreated because of their beliefs. Nor am I claiming that my beliefs will always be superior, or that a certain set of beliefs must absolutely be the 'correct way'. What I will say is that beliefs drive our very words and actions, and thus, we should always examine these beliefs to ensure that we have a solid foundation that reflects the values of Jesus. In fact, by saying that our values should reflect the values of Jesus, I am already being offensive to a large section of the population.

Jesus is one of the most tangible and obvious examples of how beliefs created an impact. He was either accepted as the Son of God or considered the most terrible of blasphemers depending on what people believed. Of course, the topic of religion is one that is so inherently offensive that it's no wonder that this is often avoided. Here's a quick refresher on the basic tenets of Christianity: We have all sinned and become separated from God. Only by accepting Jesus can we reconnect with God and enjoy eternal life. Before I became Christian, the inference of this message was that if I didn't believe what Christians believed, I'd be going to Hell to suffer eternal torment. While I dare not claim to be an authority on other religions, it should be obvious to anyone that this is inherently incompatible, even offensive, to anyone with a non-Christian set of beliefs. Likewise, if you were to ask me to be honest, I'm afraid that many non-Christian beliefs are utterly offensive to me, so much so that I'd rather the people who believe them repudiate them completely and embrace my own beliefs! Yet here we are, coexisting relatively peacefully.

For me, the danger lies in attacking the person for their beliefs. Jesus was certainly no stranger to attacks of all kinds. This is where we need to remember that another one of the foundations of Christian belief is to love others, and this includes those who don't share our beliefs. Taken to its extreme, this means that we show love even if those who disagree with us will never be won over to our side. For myself, and probably for many other people, it's just natural to bash the type of person with beliefs considered offensive. Think of racists, it's far too easy to dehumanize and caricaturize these people as below trash, and assault them as thus. It's far more challenging to treat them with respect, even if we abhor their values, knowing full well that there is a chance they may never come to see things our way. Even among fellow Christians there are a myriad of views that may clash with each other, and if being graceful to non-Christians was hard, it appears that grace among fellow Christians is in even shorter supply.

Why did I decide to start this website? I believe that perhaps I can share some useful food for thought that will help people live better lives. Whether you're Christian or not, and whether you agree with my beliefs or not, I urge you, dear reader, to be respectful of people and embrace civility. Hopefully, we'll find that even though some beliefs are utterly detestable (and perhaps, rightfully so), we can still show honor to the ones espousing them. I write this to serve as a reminder to myself just as much as all other people.
