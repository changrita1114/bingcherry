On the Topic of Names

One of the ways our words can have a significant impact.

Posted by Bing L. Cherry on March 27, 2021

There was recently an amusing piece of news from Taiwan about a sushi restaurant in Taiwan offering free, all you can eat sushi to people with the characters 'gui yu', or 'salmon', in their name. In Taiwan, the cost of changing your name is $3 and each person can change their name a maximum of 3 times in their lifetime, leading to chaos as a large multitude did exactly that in order to take advantage of this promotion. This made me think about the importance of names from a broader perspective.

Obviously, the Bible is full of numerous examples of names and their significance. Jehovah Rapha, Elohim, Yahweh are just a few of God's names. Then there are the famous name changes, such as Abram becoming Abraham, Sarai becoming Sarah, and Jacob becoming Israel. Each name change carried with it a change in the entire identity of individual. Then we get into the names of people groups, Israelites, Midianites, Romans, Egyptians, Babylonians, etc... We can see how names create identities. On the other hand, the abuse of names carries with it terrible consequences.

Matthew 5:22 "But I tell you that anyone who is angry with a brother or sister will be subject to judgment. Again, anyone who says to a brother or sister, ‘Raca,’ is answerable to the court. And anyone who says, ‘You fool!’ will be in danger of the fire of hell." It is logical to say that if insulting someone is considered a terrible sin, using insulting names would not be viewed favorably by Jesus either. You'll hear of people using words such as 'libs' 'cons', 'libtards', or 'conservaturds' to describe those whose views they disagree with. Then there's the slew of derogatory racial epiphets that I will not be mentioning here. We Christians often talk about how the ways of the world poison our mind and soul, and a culture of insults and name-calling would certainly be part of this poison. When we devalue humans with insulting names, we are falling down a dark path.

I dare not claim myself innocent of this crime, so these words are as much an exhortion to myself as it is to others, in order to remind all people of the dangers of misusing names and words. I would also urge people to be wary of any public figures who resort to insults and name-calling in general, as their words are promoting murder and endangering those who embrace their ways into the fires of hell. Instead, why not put our words into encouragement and good?
