Why Believe in God?

Some thoughts about prosperity and suffering.

Posted by Bing L. Cherry on April 24, 2021

I've been asked by some people the question: "Why should I believe in God?" The textbook answer sounds simple enough, it's because we can re-establish a relationship with God and spend eternity in paradise, free from the tortures of Hell. One might also add that it's because God is real, and that He really loves us. Of course, while these answers are certainly Biblically correct, they may feel very intangible and not necessarily persuasive for some. It got me thinking about the messages I often heard preached when I first became a Christian.

At the very first church I attended, there was often a teaching about how, once you became Christian, you would see many outward signs of God's blessings. Your relationships with people would improve, you would enjoy abundant good health, and you would see some degree of financial success. By now, most people are familiar with the 'Prosperity Gospel', and the church I attended would frequently reference certain American televangelists to justify the legitmacy of this doctrine. On the other hand, there are certainly plenty of Christians who have spoken out against this doctrine, and people need to look no further than Jesus and Paul for counterexamples in the Bible. However, I'm not here to join in the bashing of the 'Prosperity Gospel'. While I disagree with it on many levels, I understand the appeal: "You should believe in God because you will be blessed."

Another important principle of Christian life is the suffering we must endure for Jesus. The Lord himself spoke in length on the different types of persecution and turmoil Christians would face for believing in Him. Even in the old Testament, we can find plenty of examples, like the prophets who lived in the times of corrupt kings, and of the righteous suffering under the heavy arm of the wicked in general. Suffice to say, this is not exactly the most appealing message to hear for some people, but it's certainly doctrinally sound. Interestingly enough, this was also a message frequently preached at that same church I attended. You would think that the messages of suffering and prosperity would be incompatible with each other, and indeed there are those who feel as though their life became filled with nothing but trouble since they believed in God.

From a very practical standpoint, whether Christian or not, people shall experience both prosperity and suffering throughout their lifetime to varying degrees. In a very natural, human way, a Christian who follows God's teachings on love and integrity could see some real life bennefits: people will be drawn to them, and their kindness and honesty at work or in business results in financial success. Unfortunately, it's also natural that for some, becoming Christian will cause ruptures in relationships with friends and family, and there will be times that sincerely following Christ's principles result in financial hardship. There is a fundamental problem here, which is when we look at the outward results at all. 1 Samuel 16:7 says that man looks at the outward appearance, but God sees the heart. The promise of outward blessings should not entice us, nor should the suffering dissuade us from following God.

Going back to the original question, "why should we believe in God?", I realize that I would need to dig further than the 'textbook answer'. Why is someone asking this question? Do they put their hope in God providing physical, tangible rewards? Or are they afraid that by believing in God, they will have to join in the suffering? Good and bad things happen to good and bad people, and not respectively like many would hope. 1 Corinthians 15:19 says very clearly that if we're only hoping for Christ in this life, then we are to be pitied (sometimes translated to mean 'we are the most miserable'). The world we live in now is so very flawed, and to be honest, whether you are Christian or not, if this world is where we are putting our hopes, then prepare to be disappointed. Yet if you are hoping that there is something far greater, and eternal, then you should believe in God.
