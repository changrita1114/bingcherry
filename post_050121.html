Our Purpose

What is God's calling for us?

Posted by Bing L. Cherry on May 01, 2021

If you're Christian, you've probably heard many times that God has a special purpose or destiny for us. We hear about people getting called into the mission field, called to the workplace, called to build churches, etc. These are all noble pursuits and also topics that won't be covered by me. I remember that one of my first lessons at my church's school was about God caring more about who we are than about what we do. Indeed we should always be seeking out God to discern what He wants us to do, but the key to God's calling lies in something far more basic and simple.

There are two principles that are easy to remember, but really fundamental to Christians. First is the most important commandent, to love God and love people. Second, that God rewards us based on our faith, not works. 1 Corinthians 13: 1-3 is essentially saying that our actions should always come from a heart of love, otherwise the miracles we perform and the good works we do will be for naught. Wherever God calls us, and for whatever purpose He wants us there, it's important to not lose sight of this. Even non-believers have a purpose, just look at Nebuchanezzar and Cyrus. They were pagan rulers used by God for very important purposes, but few would argue that they were considered great examples of righteousness for us to follow.

The second part is more interesting. We have heard people say that God rewards us for what we have done. Salvation by faith, sanctification through works is a common teaching. There's always that danger of misunderstanding this as some sort of 'karma': that once we become Christians, we somehow must perform a quota of faithful actions in order to receive a better reward from God. At the churches I have gone to, the pastors were always saying that the difference between Christianity and other religions is that Christianity focuses on relationship and grace, while other religions are all about earning their god's favor by effort. If Christians also have to perform works to earn God's favor, then how is our faith different from that of a non-Christian? Obviously, we need to go back to 1 Corinthians 13, and then that saying about God caring more about who we are than about what we do makes sense.

Living out God's purpose for us can only come from a relationship with God built on faith and love. Whatever our calling might be, it is simply a means to that end. Performing signs and wonders, serving the poor, spreading the gospel, caring for our neighbors, the list goes on and on. All of these are outward signs of a faith filled life. At least, I pray that we can live our lives in that way. When we go out and do what God wants us to do, never lose sight of the person God wants us to be.
