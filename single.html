Respect People, not Beliefs

  • February 27, 2021
  • Opinion
  • Bing L. Cherry

First, to make things clear, I am not claiming that people should be disrespected or mistreated because of their beliefs. Nor am I claiming that my beliefs will always be superior, or that a certain set of beliefs must absolutely be the 'correct way'. What I will say is that beliefs drive our very words and actions, and thus, we should always examine these beliefs to ensure that we have a solid foundation that makes our lives, and hopefully society as a whole, a better place to live in.

One of the most tangible and obvious examples of how beliefs impact everyday life would be regarding food. What we believe about food, from nutrition, price, taste, and environmental impact, tempered by how we prioritize these aspects, affects our diet tremendously. Of course, the topic of religion is one that is so inherently offensive that it's no wonder that this topic is often avoided. As Christians, this is even more pointedly so, considering just how diverse the beliefs can be across a broad spectrum of the Christian population. Here's a quick refresher on the basic tenets of Christianity: We have all sinned and became separated from God. Only be accepting Jesus can we reconnect with God and enjoy eternal life. Before I became Christian, the inference of this message was that if I didn't believe what Christians believed, I'd be going to Hell to suffer eternal torment. While I dare not claim to be an authority on other religions, it should be obvious to anyone that this is inherently incompatible, even offensive, to anyone with a non-Christian set of beliefs. Likewise, if you were to ask me to be honest, I'm afraid that I'd have to say that frankly, many people's beliefs are quite offensive as well. Yet here we are, coexisting relatively peacefully.

For me, the danger lies in attacking the person for their beliefs. Another one of the foundations of Christian belief is to love others, and this includes those who don't share our beliefs. Taken to its extreme, this means that we show love even if those who disagree with us will never be won over to our side. For myself, and probably for many other people, it's just natural to bash that type of person. Think of racists, it's far too easy to dehumanize and caricaturize these people are below trash, and assault them as thus. It's far more challenging to treat them with respect, even if we abhor their values, knowing full well that there is a chance they may never come to see things our way. Why did I decide to start this website? I believe that perhaps I can share some useful food for thought that will help people live better lives. Whether you're Christian or not, and whether you agree with my beliefs or not, I urge you, dear reader, to be respectful of people and embrace civility.
